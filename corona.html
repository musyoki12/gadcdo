Corona Sensitization

#Empower and Transform the lives of the community.

What We Do

As the novel coronavirus (COVID-19) spreads around the world, we are stepping up and taking action to fight the disease. We, a network of youth under the age of 25, have already started sharing basic protective measures with our communities, translating vital resources into local languages, and launching campaigns to fight xenophobia and misinformation.Rather than resorting to fear or panic, our members are providing a more impactful response: informing themselves and helping others. Today we are hear, as a first step to launch one of the strategic way of mitigating the spread of the disease. We will be distributing face Mask and hand sanitizer that will target the general public. Today we are unveiling a roadmap on our efforts of educating our community on risk of contracting the disease. Under the joint of ourself and the public health office to educate our community members on the need to keep hygiene and the safe their live. Over the past month our team have been at the village level doing door to door covid19 awareness. We emphasized on the need to wear face Mask, washing hands, keeping social distancing and avoiding the crowded places.We realized the gab of little knowledge and it's our duty as a community members relay true information of the disease, we do so by 1. Stay informed and ensure others do too. As fear spreads faster than facts, inform yourself about the virus. Deepen your knowledge and point others to trusted information. This will go a long way to debunk myths and build resilience in your community. 2. Take action to keep your community safe. When you’re young and healthy, your risk of developing severe illness is lower. So consider those in your community who are most vulnerable, including older persons and persons with pre-existing medical conditions (such as high blood pressure, heart disease, lung disease, cancer or diabetes). Do your best to support them. Encourage others to take precautions, including washing your hands regularly, keeping a social distance from others and staying home when you’re sick. Basic actions like this can help limit the virus’ spread and its impact on public health, society and the economy. 3.The three Cs approach. Involves avoiding closed spaces with poor ventilation, crowded places with groups of people nearby, and close-contact settings, like one-on-one conversations in close range. The three Cs method leaves individual decisions about where to go and what kind of risks to take up to the individual, minimizing the effects of the virus while allowing life to continue, albeit in limited fashion. With this campaign to sensitize on the disease we expect the reduction on the risk of contracting the virus and commensurately flattening the curve of the disease. Their is no hierarchy in doing what's right it's our responsibility to help others and build more resilient inclusive and informed communities. .
